Emerging Science and Technology

Domain hub for spoileralert.wtf — based on Films from the Future by Andrew Maynard

About This Domain

Films from the Future explores a sweeping range of emerging sciences and technologies through the lens of twelve science fiction movies. This domain maps the key technologies and scientific concepts covered in the book, organized as individual topic pages that can be browsed, searched, and cross-referenced with the other domains on the site.

Each topic page introduces the technology or scientific concept, explains how the book uses a specific film to explore it, and connects it to real-world developments. The tone is accessible and engaging — this is not a textbook, but a guide for curious readers who want to understand what these technologies are and why they matter.

These technologies don't exist in isolation — they converge, interact, and amplify each other in ways that are often hard to predict. The cross-links on each topic page connect to ethical themes in the Responsible and Ethical Innovation domain and broader frameworks in the Navigating the Future domain.

  • Source: Chapter 2 — Jurassic Park
  • Scope: Using genetic engineering to bring extinct species back to life. Covers the science of extracting and reconstructing ancient DNA, the technical barriers, and the ecological implications. Connects to real-world efforts like the woolly mammoth revival.

  • Page: est_genetic_engineering.md
  • Source: Chapter 2 — Jurassic Park, Chapter 11 — Inferno, Chapter 12 — The Day After Tomorrow
  • Scope: The broader field of manipulating genetic code, including CRISPR-Cas9 gene editing. Covers applications from modifying organisms to editing human embryos. Distinct from de-extinction — this is about the foundational capability.
  • Cross-links: Dual-Use Research and Biosecurity; Could We? Should We?

3. Cloning and Reproductive Biology

  • Page: est_cloning.md
  • Source: Chapter 3 — Never Let Me Go
  • Scope: Creating genetically identical organisms, from Dolly the sheep to the fictional human clones of Never Let Me Go. Covers therapeutic vs. reproductive cloning, IVF as context, and the distinction between what's technically possible and what's ethically acceptable.
  • Cross-links: Human Dignity and What Makes Us Human; Too Valuable to Fail

4. Synthetic Biology

  • Page: est_synthetic_biology.md
  • Source: Chapter 9 — Transcendence, Chapter 11 — Inferno
  • Scope: Engineering biology with the precision of designing computer systems. Covers the convergence of biology and engineering, synthetic genome construction, and the ability to design organisms from scratch.
  • Cross-links: Dual-Use Research and Biosecurity; Technological Convergence

5. Gain-of-Function Research

  • Page: est_gain_of_function.md
  • Source: Chapter 11 — Inferno
  • Scope: Deliberately making pathogens more dangerous in order to study them — one of the most controversial areas in modern biology. Covers the H5N1 flu experiments, the publication debate, and the tension between scientific knowledge and biosecurity.
  • Cross-links: Dual-Use Research and Biosecurity; The Role of Scientists and Innovators

6. Gene Drives

  • Page: est_gene_drives.md
  • Source: Chapter 2 — Jurassic Park, Chapter 11 — Inferno
  • Scope: Technologies that can push genetic modifications through entire wild populations. A powerful tool for disease control (e.g., malaria-carrying mosquitoes) but with profound ecological risks if the modification spreads beyond its intended target.
  • Cross-links: Complexity and Unintended Consequences; Could We? Should We?
