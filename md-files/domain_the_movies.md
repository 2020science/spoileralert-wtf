# The Movies

*Domain hub for spoileralert.wtf — based on Films from the Future by Andrew Maynard*

---

## About This Domain

Each chapter of Films from the Future uses a specific science fiction movie as the starting point for exploring emerging technologies and their social consequences. This domain provides a page for each film — the entry point for readers who come to the site through a movie they love (or love to hate).

The movie pages are not film reviews. They use each movie as a springboard for exploring the technologies and themes covered in the corresponding chapter, while providing enough context about the film itself for readers who haven't seen it. The book is explicit that scientific accuracy isn't the point — these movies matter because of what they reveal about our relationship with technology, not because they get the science right.

The films connect to the technologies explored in [Emerging Science and Technology](https://spoileralert.wtf/md-files/domain_emerging_science_and_technology.md), the ethical tensions in [Responsible and Ethical Innovation](https://spoileralert.wtf/md-files/domain_responsible_and_ethical_innovation.md), and the broader frameworks in [Navigating the Future](https://spoileralert.wtf/md-files/domain_navigating_the_future.md). Every film page routes readers into the other three domains based on what interests them.

## Bookend References

These two films frame the book but don't have dedicated movie pages:

### 2001: A Space Odyssey (1968)
- **Chapter:** [Chapter 1 — In the Beginning](https://spoileralert.wtf/md-files/ch01_in_the_beginning.md)
- **Role:** The film that sparked the author's lifelong interest in the intersection of science fiction and technological futures. HAL's famous refusal — "I'm sorry, Dave, I'm afraid I can't do that" — is the book's opening line.

### The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy (2005)
- **Chapter:** [Chapter 14 — Looking to the Future](https://spoileralert.wtf/md-files/ch14_looking_to_the_future.md)
- **Role:** The closing metaphor. Like Adams' fictional Guide, Films from the Future is an incomplete guide to a vast and complex subject — but its most important advice is the same: "Don't Panic."

## How This Domain Connects

This is one of six interconnected domains on spoileralert.wtf:

- **[Emerging Science and Technology](https://spoileralert.wtf/md-files/domain_emerging_science_and_technology.md)** — the 21 technologies explored in the book
- **[Responsible and Ethical Innovation](https://spoileralert.wtf/md-files/domain_responsible_and_ethical_innovation.md)** — 13 cross-cutting ethical themes that recur across multiple chapters and technologies
- **[Navigating the Future](https://spoileralert.wtf/md-files/domain_navigating_the_future.md)** — 12 frameworks for thinking about technology and society
- **[The Movies](https://spoileralert.wtf/md-files/domain_the_movies.md)** (this file) — 12 film pages connecting each movie to the technologies and themes it illuminates
- **[Post-2018 Developments](https://spoileralert.wtf/md-files/domain_post_2018_developments.md)** — 20 technologies and developments that emerged or accelerated after the book was published
- **[Complex Emerging Questions](https://spoileralert.wtf/md-files/domain_complex_emerging_questions.md)** — 16 contested questions at the intersection of technology, society, and the future

Film pages are the most likely entry point for casual browsers — each one is designed to be engaging and inviting, routing readers into the other three domains based on what interests them. These aren't the "best" sci-fi movies; they're movies that open up specific conversations about technology and society.

The full book text is available in chapter files ([Chapter 1](https://spoileralert.wtf/md-files/ch01_in_the_beginning.md) through [Chapter 14](https://spoileralert.wtf/md-files/ch14_looking_to_the_future.md) plus [acknowledgments](https://spoileralert.wtf/md-files/ch15_acknowledgments.md)). For guidance on tone and approach, see [usage_guidance.md](https://spoileralert.wtf/md-files/usage_guidance.md).
